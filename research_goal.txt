# ODE Multi-Model Latent Space Research - Research Goal

## Core Research Question
How can multiple models of the SAME EXACT KIND, TYPE, AND SIZE work together at inference time to get a significantly better response right off the bat, without using reasoning approaches?

## Approach
Instead of reasoning/workflow approaches, focus on **dynamically adjusting vectors within latent space** during inference using an ODE-like approach.

## Key Ideas to Explore
1. Derive the source vector and observe where it goes / what shape it traces
2. Spot the same vector across multiple models and adjust weights within one model by comparing with vectors from another model ("learning at inference")
3. Other novel techniques for inference-time collaboration

## Technical Goals
- Scale reasoning/refinement effort during inference itself
- Enable the system to learn at the same time during inference
- Support combining hundreds of models (like a corpus callosum)
- Use diffusion text models such as LLADA and DreamLM dLLMs

## What NOT to Use
- No reasoning approaches (ReAct, chain-of-thought, etc.)
- No multi-turn refinement waiting for full workflow completion

## Success Criteria
A system where multiple identical models dynamically share and adjust latent vectors at inference time to produce superior outputs compared to any single model working alone.
